Research

I am an associate professor at the National Research Centre (NRC) Cairo, Egypt, with a Ph.D. in Analytical Chemistry from Ain Shams University. My research focuses on the development of electrochemical/bio-sensors, including potentiometric and voltammetric techniques. I specialize in nano-materials based electrochemical sensors, including carbon nanotubes, gold nanoparticles, graphene oxide, and graphene. I also synthesize organic compounds such as ionophores and polymers for sensor applications.

Publications

  • Polymeric Membrane Sensors for Batch and Flow Injection Potentiometric Determination of Procainamide - IEEE SENSORS JOURNAL, 2021
  • Synthesis and characterization of nanostructured copper and lanthanum co-doped zirconia for voltammetric sensing of tumor biomarkers - Electrochemical Science Advances, 2021
  • Voltammetric determination of Marbofloxacin at carbon paste sensor integrated with copper oxide nanoparticles - Electroanalysis, 2022
  • Voltammetric determination terbinafine at carbon paste electrode modified with graphene nanosheets - Egypt. J. Chem., 2023

Teaching Experience

I have extensive experience in teaching Analytical Chemistry courses. I served as an Assistant Professor at Al-Ghad International Colleges for Applied Medical Sciences in Abha, Saudi Arabia, from 2015 to 2017. Prior to that, I was an Instructor at Helwan University, where I taught Analytical Chemistry to undergraduate students from 2011 to 2015.

Collaborations

I have been involved in numerous international research collaborations, including projects with Purdue University, Universitat Autonoma de Barcelona, and Bucharest University. Notable projects include the development of micro/nanosensors for early cancer detection and the creation of novel electrochemical nano-sensors for toxic ion detection.
